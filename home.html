The work very interestingly shows the life of a young man from another culture, with a difficult family life and an uncertain future. There are both positive and negative personalities in his life, each of which affects him in a special way. Thus, in the boy’s soul there is a struggle for his worldview, a struggle in which there can be no intermediate way out other than final victory - on the one hand, Nuur’s teacher, who sees in the boy a talented student and a worthy member of society in the future, and on the other, an imam , which inclines the boy to a radical interpretation of Islam and directs him to fight against the society in which he grew up.

Author:

  • Hassan Ghedi Santur is a Somali-Canadian writer and journalist. He has a unique background, having been born in Somalia, raised in Kenya, and later immigrating to Canada. His experiences as an immigrant and his multicultural upbringing are often reflected in his works.
  • Santur's writing explores themes of identity, displacement, and the complex interplay between cultures. He is known for addressing the challenges faced by Somali immigrants, especially the youth, as they navigate their identity in a new cultural context.
  • "The Youth of God" is one of Santur's notable works, delving into the life of a Somali-Canadian protagonist and exploring the broader implications of radicalization, cultural clash, and the search for identity. His writings contribute to the understanding of the Somali diaspora and the intricacies of cultural integration.
